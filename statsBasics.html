4  Statistical Notation

4.1 Sums

\(\sum_{i=1}^{3}{X_i} = X_1 + X_2 + X_3\)

Example:

The equation

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} \hat{p} &= \frac{\sum\limits_N{Cr(r-1)}}{\sum\limits_N{Cr(s-1)}}\\ \end{split} \end{equation}\]

would be more clearly written as

\[\begin{equation} \begin{split} \hat{p} &= \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^N{C_ir_i(r_i-1)}}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^N{C_ir_i(s_i-1)}}\\ \end{split} \end{equation}\]

4.2 Products

\(\prod_{i=1}^{3}{X_i} = X_1 \times X_2 \times X_3\)

4.3 Conditional Probability

  1. Definition:

\(P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}\)

  1. Let \(S = \bigcup\limits_{i=1}^nA_i\) and \(A_i \cap A_j = \emptyset\) for \(i \ne j\). Then \(P(B) = \sum\limits_{i=1}^nP(B|A_i)P(A_i)\)

  2. \(P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}\)

  3. Bayes Rule

Let \(S = \bigcup\limits_{i=1}^nA_i\) and \(A_i \cap A_j = \emptyset\) for \(i \ne j\). Then \(P(A_j|B) =\frac{P(B|A_j)P(A_j)}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^nP(B|A_i)P(A_i)}\)

  1. Intermediate Conditioning

\(P(A|B) = \sum\limits_{i}P(A|X_i,B)P(X_i|B)\)

  1. \(P(A|B, C) = \frac{P(B|A,C)P(A|C)}{P(B|C)}\)

  2. \(P(A,B|C) = P(A|B,C)P(B|C)\)

  3. If \(A_i\) are mutually exclusive and exhaustive, then

\(P(A_j|B, C) = \frac{P(B|A_j,C)P(A_j|C)}{\sum\limits_iP(B|A_i,C)P(A_i|C)}\)
